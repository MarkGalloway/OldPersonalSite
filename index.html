Preliminary DashAB Compiler

Under the guidance of Professor Nelson Amaral of the University of Alberta, a team including myself and three other students created a compiler for DashAB, a subset of the Dash language currently under development by IBM.

Our team created this compiler using an Antlr parser and hand-built tree walkers in Java. These tree walkers performed operator promotion and optimization logic such as constant propagation. The output of our compiler is functional LLVM IR.

University of Alberta Campus Maps

A group of fellow students and I developed this project as a prototype campus-wide pathfinding application. It was built on top of the existing Campus Maps exterior pathfinding framework but adds interior pathfinding solutions for an overall goal of combining the most effective interior and exterior path. It was designed as a responsive HTML5/CSS3/JQuery site allowing for quick access on multiple form factors.

Applications Intern - Pason Systems Inc. (May 2013 - August 2014)

I did a 16 month internship at Pason Systems as a part of the Industrial Internship Practicum at the University of Alberta. I worked with a team of thirteen fellow software developers and software engineers following Agile Scrum practices.

At Pason, I participated in many different phases of the product development cycle, including design, testing, field trials, and maintenance. My main role at Pason was to maintain the C#/C++ Windows applications which are deployed on drilling rig workstations worldwide. Issues often arose in the field, often not fixable by a simple patch, which taught me the incredible importance of extensive testing before release. Nearing the end of my term with Pason, I participated in the development of an automated testing framework using Ranorex, which would preform GUI testing of applications following many possible flows of execution.

I learned many valuable lessons at Pason, such as how to approach your team with proposals for process changes in order to improve coordination efficiency. For example, I proposed a sweeping change to the process of creating Windows images which improved image creation efficiency and removed conflicting updates. This new process was still used after my internship was over.
